## Page: Home (`/`)

### Hero / Mission Statement

Inspired by Bryan Stevenson's call "to get proximate" to the poor, the incarcerated, and those condemned to die on death row, New York Avenue Presbyterian Church partnered with the Public Defender Service (PDS) of the District of Columbia in 2018 and established the Returning Citizens Assistance Network (RCAN). RCAN is an informal network of concerned and caring individuals and congregations in DC, MD, and VA committed to social justice.

RCAN focuses on resolving problems that threaten the ability of formerly incarcerated individuals — or those still involved with the criminal justice system — to successfully transition to the community. All individuals are referred from the DC Public Defender Service; all are people PDS represents or has previously represented.

## Page: About (`/about`)

### Mission & Origin

In 2018, New York Avenue Presbyterian Church and Metropolitan AME Church worked with the Public Defender Service of the District of Columbia to establish the Returning Citizens Assistance Network (RCAN). This informal network is composed of twenty congregations that agree to assist returning citizens and others in the criminal justice system who face specific short-term needs.

RCAN generally engages with one or two cases each week, with that number growing as more congregations and individuals join. RCAN shares information about each case with the full network; each congregation then determines for itself whether it is able to help with a particular case.

### Holiday Gift Program

In December 2020, RCAN began a gift drive gathering gifts for select families of justice-impacted clients and delivering them by Christmas. RCAN continues to prepare holiday gift baskets each year for PDS-selected families and individuals.

### The RCAN Fund

The RCAN Fund was established through New York Avenue Presbyterian Church in January 2021 to facilitate online donations supporting client requests from the DC PDS. In 2025, contributions helped 361 clients — including 10 families (51 adults and children) who received holiday gift baskets, and 36 incarcerated clients without external support who each received $50 in commissary funds.

### How the Network Works

Each congregation that joins RCAN agrees to:

- Identify one or more members as contact persons for that congregation
- Share information about the network with members and identify individuals who wish to help
- Receive regular updates about specific individuals who need assistance
- Check with congregation members to see if anyone can assist with a request
- Help coordinate efforts with persons who request assistance

Any congregation that wishes to join RCAN or has questions should contact **email@example.org**.

## Page: How We Help (`/how-we-help`)

### Types of Assistance

RCAN supports PDS clients with a variety of critical needs, including:

- Help finding or furnishing an apartment, or paying an application fee or security deposit
- Free food, clothing, and items for personal hygiene
- Transportation assistance — Metro cards, free bicycles, Uber rides
- Funds to avoid eviction or help with other emergency financial needs, including utilities
- Assistance looking for work and/or learning job skills
- Informal life-skills counseling or personal mentoring
- Sending books to incarcerated clients
- Assisting with the costs for PDS juveniles to participate in therapeutic programs as part of release conditions
- Court-imposed fees and sanctions that PDS clients cannot afford to pay
- Providing a letter of support for PDS clients seeking release under the Incarceration Reduction Amendment Act
- Green card fees, therapy sessions, and cellphones to keep in contact with attorneys and/or find work

### Program Highlights

**Beauty Behind Bars: The Transformative Power of Arts in Confinement**
RCAN partnered with DC PDS to host a gallery exhibition featuring work by more than a dozen currently and formerly incarcerated artists at The Studio Gallery. Two RCAN churches — St. Mark's and St. Columba's — later hosted the exhibition in April and September respectively. The exhibit was successfully used by PDS attorneys to win the freedom of artist clients seeking release after decades of incarceration under the Incarceration Reduction Amendment Act.

### Prison Friendship Project

About 4,000 people from Washington, DC are scattered in federal prisons across the country. Most are incarcerated hundreds of miles from their families, in conditions of isolation and deprivation that are hard to imagine. Many have served long sentences and lost contact with family and friends. A human connection from the outside offers hope, affirms their humanity, and helps set the stage for successful reentry.

To increase personal connections between RCAN supporters and DC PDS clients who are currently incarcerated, RCAN created the Prison Friendship Project. Any individual — or small group — who would like to become a "Prison Friend" will be matched with a PDS client serving a sentence in the Bureau of Prisons.

**What Prison Friends do:**

- Learn about the background and current situation of the person they are matched with
- Build a relationship through letters and reading materials
- Give gifts on special occasions to the person's commissary account — providing access to food, drinks, toiletries, and personal items
- Provide assistance in any other mutually agreed-upon way as the relationship develops

RCAN supports Prison Friends with background on the client, access to lawyers and social workers, guidelines for writing letters and sending items, and ideas for correspondence.

In 2025, the Prison Friendship Project created **23 new pen pal matches**. Currently **40 clients** receive regular communication through the project.

Anyone who supports RCAN is invited to become a Prison Friend or organize a small Prison Friend group. A group provides more resources, but a single committed person can be just as meaningful.
